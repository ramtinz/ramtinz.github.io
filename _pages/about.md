Since completing my PhD in biomedical engineering in Denmark, I’ve applied my engineering background across multiple industries, with a strong focus on building AI and machine learning solutions from research to production. I bring over a decade of experience spanning applied research, software engineering, data science, and MLOps, and I specialize in translating complex technical challenges into scalable systems, reliable pipelines, and business impact. I enjoy working at the intersection of engineering execution and strategic problem-solving, whether in technology-driven teams or regulated environments such as healthcare and insurance.

Throughout my career, I have led and contributed to the design and development of multiple software tools and end-to-end data platforms, working with diverse data types and environments. I have extensive experience in Python and R, modern cloud-based infrastructure, and building efficient pipelines and workflows that support production-grade analytics and AI. In the AI/ML domain, I have worked across the full lifecycle, from problem framing and model design to deployment and monitoring, including deep learning and generative AI such as large language models (LLMs) and agentic AI.

While my early work focused heavily on biomedical research, analyzing biosignals, behavior, and multi-modal biological data where innovation is impactful, I’m comfortable operating in environments where reliability, compliance, risk awareness, and traceability matter. My recent career as an MLOps engineer in insurtech with successful ML/AI software module implementations to handle claims automatically and accurately for thousands of customers in Denmark, Switzerland, and Norway is a solid example. I also bring hands-on engineering experience from experimental and lab environments, including hardware-integrated systems, robotics, and simulation-based development. This has given me a strong systems-thinking mindset and the ability to reason across software, data, and operational constraints.

As a key part of my career, especially during my postdoc, I’ve developed strong leadership and project management capabilities through cross-functional collaboration with researchers, engineers, and stakeholders from Australia and Asia to Europe and the US. I can contribute to roles that involve advising teams, driving technical direction, mentoring, and aligning execution with strategic goals. I’m passionate about ideation and prototyping, and I enjoy taking initiatives from early-stage exploration to robust, real-world impact.

I have contributed to the following research areas (see the <a href="https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=3-pFVNQAAAAJ&hl=en&oi=ao" target="_blank">full list of my publications here</a>):

- **Medical AI and personalized medicine**
During my postdoc at the Center of Excellence for Health, Immunity and Infections, I worked on projects with the aim of releasing scientific software solutions that can be used in research to approach clinical applications for ML prediction models, with a focus on model transparency and explainability. See the **Medical AI Toolbox (MAIT)** ([GitHub](https://github.com/PERSIMUNE/MAIT)) and the **ExplaineR package** ([GitHub](https://github.com/PERSIMUNE/explainer)). These software solutions have been used for the development and validation of predictive models for many challenging diseases, in which the results have revealed novel evidence to clinical researchers and have provided the foundations and toolsets for future implementations and further validations. Please refer to the published scientific papers in my publication list for more information.

- **Human factors and cognitive science**
It was mostly during my PhD fellowship that I worked on projects with the aim of finding objective measures of mental fatigue based on eye movement dynamics. The eyes, and specifically their movements, are a great source of information on many cognitive processes and psychological states. In my PhD studies, I discovered new metrics based on eye movements that were effective in objectively measuring fatigue from computer work, and based on those findings, I developed a proof-of-concept feedback system based on a real-time machine learning model to mitigate fatigue while doing computer work. This research has been published and is among my highly cited research papers.

My research in cognitive science was also focused on developing methods to better explore the data used for cognitive processes such as memory recall, in which I worked on signal processing methods to extract relevant biological information.

- **Infectious diseases and the human gut microbiome**
After a few months of my postdoc, the COVID-19 pandemic occurred, and since I was working at the bioinformatics group of a research center focused on infectious diseases, the pandemic naturally received great research attention. I also learned a lot about some of the major infectious diseases and important issues and concepts surrounding them, such as personalized medicine, resistance to antibiotics, the human gut microbiome and how it can affect both general health and disease outcomes, and the technologies and procedures involved in obtaining clinical data, especially multi-omics such as metagenomics, proteomics, etc. One of the biggest challenges in bioinformatics when it comes to dealing with such a variety of data is to have a solid understanding of the data and to understand the challenges and limitations of the available methods and tools to analyze such complex data to provide impactful and practical information that could be used for the treatment of patients. There has been tremendous progress in bioinformatics to test and validate hypotheses beyond traditional biostatistics. It was a great opportunity for me to contribute to scientific research in infectious diseases and the human gut microbiome using state-of-the-art technologies and novel methods.
